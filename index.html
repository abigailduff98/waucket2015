Our Mission

One in every nine people around the world do not have access to safe and clean water for usage. Over 4 million people die annually due to water-related diseases. Our mission is to educate the public about this rapidly growing struggle in which various parts of the world live through. Hopefully through our product, our audience will become involved in the cause and do their part to contribute to the “WAUCKET” movement. Waucket was created to raise awareness about the scarcity of sanitary water in developing countries.

Our project is designed as a tool to educate the general public, teaching them of the living conditions of people who lack such basic needs as sanitary water. There are a vast number of countries that do not have access to this resource. Clean water is a basic necessity to maintain life. As explorer Jean-Michel Cousteau once said, “Clean water, the essence of life and a birthright for everyone, must become available to all people now.”





Charities

Charity:Water

A non-profit organization that provides clean and safe drinking water to developing nations. People can start their own campaign to help raise money for the cause. Learn how you can do your part to help on their website!

Water for People

Water for People brings together local entrepreneurs, civil society and communities to establish solutions that allow people to build and maintain their own reliable safe water systems. People can donate through the website or start their own fundraiser to help. Learn More!

  • Water for People

Water.org

Water.org is an American non-profit development aid organization resulting from the merger between H20 Africa. They work with in-country partners to access safe water and sanitation. They are committed to finding solutions that respond to the specific needs of each community. Learn More!

  • Water.org

The Water Project

The Water Project, a non-profit organization that is providing sustainable water projects to communities in sub-Saharan Africa who suffer from a lack of access to clean water and proper sanitation. By funding local NGO's with in-country presence, they help maintain resources. Learn more!
